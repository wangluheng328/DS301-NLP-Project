By 2008, more than 25 percent of major league baseball players were born in Latin America.
At 19, Miguel "Sugar" Santos, a serious kid from the Dominican Republic, signs with Kansas City.
He flies to Phoenix for tryouts and is sent to the Class A team "The Swing" in the fictional town of Bridgetown, Iowa, where he lives with a farm family.
Thus begins his odyssey: leaving his mom and girlfriend; living in an alien culture; learning English; overcoming jitters; working hard; achieving early success; navigating friendships, occasional racism, and a woman's mixed signals; dealing with an injury; trying performance-enhancing drugs; and, searching for his place in the world.
Will he make it to the Majors; will he play in New York?
